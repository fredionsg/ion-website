About us.

The Institute of Neurodiversity, Singapore is committed to transforming the lives of neurominorities in Singapore by fostering Awareness, Representation, Research, Connections, Collaborations and Celebrations.

The organization aims to create systemic change, social cohesion and social mobility that fosters acceptance, support, and empowerment for individuals with neurodiverse traits. The plan emphasizes collaboration with businesses, educational institutions, government agencies, and the wider community to drive sustainable impact.

Mission

To empower neurominorities through Education, Mentorship, Advocacy, Social Cohesion and Community Support.

Vision

A world where neurominorities are understood, supported, celebrated and thriving in all aspects of life.

Vision Statement

Inclusion, Empowerment, Representation, Awareness, Collaboration, and Research.

ION Caregivers Circle

Caring for a neurodivergent child, youth, or adult often means carrying a lot quietly. The planning, advocacy, and emotional labour rarely pause.

Many caregivers put careers, independence, and parts of themselves on hold to support someone they love, while holding an unspoken question about the future.

The ION Caregivers Circle is a monthly online space created for caregivers to pause, connect, and be heard without judgement or expectations.

  • A safe, judgement-free space
  • No pressure to share — listening is welcome
  • Grounded in lived experience and mutual respect

Come as you are. Share, listen, or simply sit with others who understand.

Who We Support

We proudly support and serve a diverse range of neurominorities, fostering understanding and inclusion.

Why Support is Important:

Educational Gaps

  • Insufficient training for educators to support neuro minority students.
  • Limited access to assistive technologies and tailored learning programs.

Workplace Barriers

  • Rigid hiring processes that do not accommodate neuro minority strengths.
  • Lack of workplace accommodations (e.g., quiet spaces, flexible working hours).

Social Stigma & Awareness

  • Misconceptions about neurodiverse abilities, leading to exclusion.
  • Lack of community understanding and public awareness.

Policy & Systemic Gaps

  • Limited national policies that directly support neurodiverse employment and education.
  • Limited data and statistics on neurodiverse individuals in Singapore.

Education and Awareness

01

Experiential Workshop

A hands-on experience that helps participants develop a deeper understanding of neurodiverse challenges and strengths.

This workshop encourages empathy, self-reflection, and discussions on how to build a more inclusive environment

02

Certification Programs

Introduce a "Neurodiversity-Friendly" workplace and school certification to recognize institutions implementing inclusive practices.

This is in line with certifying neuro inclusive spaces in Singapore

03

Neurodiversity Awareness Campaign

Run an annual national awareness campaign in collaboration with mainstream media and digital platforms.

Support and Mentorship:

01

Workplace Mentorship

Implement a structured mentorship program pairing neurominority employees with professionals in their respective industries for career guidance and workplace adaptation support.

02

Student Mentorship

Create buddy systems in schools where neurominority students receive peer-to-peer guidance from trained mentors. This can be in alignment with ION's reverse mentoring

03

Support Networks

Establish online and offline peer-support groups to provide safe spaces for neurominorities to share experiences and resources.

Community Engagement

01

Online Platforms:

Develop an interactive online forum where neurominorities and allies can share knowledge, experiences, and support.

02

Networking Events:

Host periodic meetups, panel discussions, and social events to create a sense of belonging within the community.

03

Creative Showcases:

Organize exhibitions featuring neurominority talents in art, music, and theater.

Policy and Advocacy

01

Legislative Engagement:

Work with policymakers and similar advocacy groups to recommend inclusive workplace policies and educational support systems.

02

Parental and Caregiver Advocacy:

Develop programs to empower caregivers with knowledge and resources to support neurodiverse individuals -priority for local government schools and international schools if required for support of local communities.

03

Inclusive Hiring Campaign:

Partner with government and institutions for employer awareness campaign promoting neurodiverse hiring and workplace adjustments.
